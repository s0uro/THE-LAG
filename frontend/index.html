About the project

Neural Reading
Intelligence Lab

THE-LAG is a university research platform for predicting reading cognitive load using eye-tracking and behavioral data. It combines XGBoost and Multi-Layer Perceptron models to classify reading difficulty, with full SHAP explainability so researchers can understand what drives each prediction.

How it works

From raw data to
deep insight

01 — Upload
Dataset Ingestion
Upload your eye-tracking or finger-tracking Excel file from the lab.
hover to reveal
01 — Upload
Accepts .xlsx or .csv with columns like FFD, TRT, isReg, dt. Saved to project root and passed directly into the pipeline.
02 — Process
Preprocessing Pipeline
Automatic data cleaning, interpolation and feature engineering.
hover to reveal
02 — Process
Runs preprocessing.py — removes outliers, interpolates FFD/FPD/TRT, computes Lag from dt, normalises len and freq, creates binary target.
03 — Train
Dual Model Training
XGBoost and MLP trained in parallel on your data.
hover to reveal
03 — Train
XGBoost (300 estimators, depth 5) and MLP (128→64→32, Adam, early stopping). Stratified 80/20 split. Models saved as .joblib files.
04 — Explain
SHAP Explainability
Understand what drives every prediction with SHAP values.
hover to reveal
04 — Explain
TreeExplainer on XGBoost. Outputs shap_summary.png (dot plot, top 15 features) and a dependence plot for the most important feature.
